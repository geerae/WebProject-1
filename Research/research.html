Copyright, Creative Common (CC) Licenses, Fair Use

The Act gives the owner of a copyright the exclusive right to do and authorize others to do certain things in regard to a copyrighted work, including: make copies, distribute the work, display or perform the work publicly, and create derivative works.
4 types of CC licenses:
1. Attribution (BY) - BY” refers to attribution. This means in order to use the work, you must attribute the author of the work. All of the Creative Commons licenses require this condition
2. Share Alike (SA) - Share Alike means that if you create an adaptation of a work that has this license, the adaptation must be licensed under the same or a compatible license.
3. NonCommercial (NC) - NC means that the work may only be used for non-commercial purpose. In NC licenses, non-commercial is defined as “not primarily intended for or directed towards commercial advantage or monetary compensation”
4. No Derivatives (ND) - ND means that you cannot share an adaptation of the work, though you could use and share it in its original form.

Web Performance & Maintenance

A general strategy is to:
-Make your files as small as possible
-Reduce the number of HTTP requests made as much as possible
-Employ clever loading techniques (such as preload) to make files available sooner
-Only load assets when they are actually needed (this is called lazy loading)
-Continue to load in the background while the user gets on with primary tasks
Maintenance:
-Mark a time on the calendar to perform usability testing once a year
-The best way to keep your domain name (and website by extension) safe is to set up auto-renew
-Update Your Header, Footer, and Legal Policies
-So at least once every couple of months, have someone in the company make a few test purchases to see how the process works
-Check for Security Updates

Web Security


1. Phishing: Fake official emails link to fake websites, where users log in, and give their passwords
2. DDoS: a network of computers overload a server with data, shutting down
3. Man-in-the-Middle Attack: hackers insert themselves between yout computer and web server
4. Cross-Site Scripting: injects malicious code into a website whick targets the visitor's browser
5. SQL Injection Attack: corrupts data to make a server divulge data, such as credit cards numbers and usernames
Prevention:
-Identity and access management (IAM)
-A comprehensive data security platform
-Security information and event management (SIEM)
-Offensive and defensive security services and threat intelligence
